Apply for funding from the UK Shared Prosperity Fund (Employment)

The employment portion of the UK Shared Prosperity Fund provides funding to organisations to provide tailored support to help people who struggle to access mainstream employment provision and to support them move closer to, or into, employment.

How the Fund works

The Fund covers all areas of the UK. Each area has its own invitation that lists the needs and priorities being addressed in that area.

Organisations can apply for funding through an invitation.

Who can apply

Any organisation able to deliver a project that meets the needs and priorities of the area.

You must have a UK bank account.

How to apply

You can apply for funding through an invitation. Your project must respond to the needs and priorities listed in the invitation.

Find an invitation for UKSPF on GOV.UK.

How much money you can apply for

The invitation tells you how much money you can apply for.

When do you need to apply by

Each invitation will have a deadline. You will need to check the invitation you are applying for.

What you need to provide

You must tell us:

  • the reference number of the invitation you are applying for
  • your Companies House reference number or charity reference number, if you have one, or another accredited body that you are registered with
  • how much you are applying for
  • the needs and priorities from the invitation you are addressing in your application
  • what you will use the money for
  • how you will manage the money
  • how you will manage the performance of your project
  • your organisation name and address
  • application contact details
  • your organisation bank account details

DWP performs due diligence checks on all organisations using the Cabinet Office Spotlight tool.

Start

Before you start

Check which invitation you are applying for before you start your application.

After you have applied

We will email your organisation with a decision on the application. We will use the email address given in the application.
